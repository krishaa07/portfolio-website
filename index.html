About

A disciplined eye
for what goes wrong.

I'm an IT Security student at Islington College with a focus on the intersection of quality assurance and offensive security. My approach is methodical — I document everything, reproduce every defect clearly, and always consider the security implications of what I find.

During my internship at YAJ Tech, I moved between QA and penetration testing — running OWASP-aligned assessments and writing structured defect reports that developers could act on immediately. I learned that good security testing and good QA share the same DNA: patience, precision, and clear communication.

Outside of technical work, I've published research on cybersecurity policy in Nepal's banking sector, and I actively contribute through AIESEC and community outreach — because the most resilient systems are built by people who understand the world they're protecting.
